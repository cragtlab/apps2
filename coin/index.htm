Singapore Coin Hunting Guide

Quick-reference list for high-value targets in your pocket change.

🪙 1978 Merlion $1 ~S$100+

  • Series: First Series (Marine/Animals)
  • Key Feature: Large, heavy coin featuring the iconic Merlion.
  • Why: Extremely low mintage year makes this the holy grail of standard $1 coins.

🪙 1989 Flora 5¢ ~S$10 - S$15

  • Series: Second Series (Floral)
  • Key Feature: Small orange-bronze coin with the Monstera plant.
  • Why: A sudden drop in production that year created a massive supply shortage.

🪙 1967 Lionfish 50¢ ~S$10 - S$20

  • Series: First Series (Marine/Animals)
  • Key Feature: Wide coin featuring a stylized Lionfish.
  • Why: The historical, first-ever year of independent Singaporean currency.

⚠️ Any Major Mint Error S$300 - S$1,000+

  • Series: Any Series
  • Key Feature: Off-center stamps, missing outer rings, or blank faces.
  • Why: Factory defects that accidentally escaped the mint are prized by premium collectors.
